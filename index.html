Souks & Workshops tour

Itinerary –

The aim of the tour is to discover the different souks of the medina and know the different fabrications of Moroccan craftsmanship. I will take you between the small alleys from the tanneries district to the famous souk "Semarine" considered as a great exhibition of artisanal production in Marrakech.
You will browse the main souks of the medina such as the Dyer's Souk, slipper & leather souk, carpenters, ironworkers, ceramic & tiles, jewelry, spices, slippers, clothing etc.
In each souk, you will make a stop in a craft workshop to discover the nature of the work done in each trade, present the Maalems (master craftsmen), see closely the different stages of production and know the old tools still used in each trade. I will explain the history of some existing symbols in handicrafts and what they represent in the local culture. In some workshops, you can participate in the manufacturing process under the assistance of Maalem.

Marrakech Highlights
Full-Day City Tour

Itinerary –

Marrakech City Tour half day : Accompanied by an experienced guide, explore Marrakech's historic landmarks including Bahia Palace, Madrassa Ben Youssef, and the Majorelle Garden. Discover the bustling souks and the exotic Medina.
Monuments and Palaces: Visit the famous Koutoubia Tower (11th century), Saadian tombs (16th century), Bahia Palace (19th century) & Medersan Ben Youssef (15th century).
Djemaa el Fna square: Really no more than an open space in the centre of the city, but the stage for a long-established ritual in which shifting circles of onlookers gather round groups of acrobats, drummers, pipe musicians, dancers, storytellers, comedians and fairground acts etc
Souks: Visit the Souks and artisans workshops inside the medina, and also discover vibrant markets full of souvenirs & goods, from from fresh vegetables and spices to leather and jewelry.

Marrakech gardens

Itinerary –

The gardens are present everywhere in Marrakech, where the marrakchis celebrated the arrival of the spring season every year by organizing picnics throughout the season, a ritual inherited for centuries.
Among the famous gardens that remain today: Menara gardens, Le Jardin Secret, Mly Abdesslam, Parc El Harti Gardens, Koutoubia gardens , to which the Majorelle garden adds an exotic charm.
During the tour, you will enjoy a leisurely ride in a Horse-Drawn Carriage , exploring the Ramparts and the nine gates that dominate this medieval city with their characteristic red stone walls. The ride will take us to Majorelle Garden. These botanical gardens are now a haven of peace in the city of Marrakech with fountains, pools, bamboo forests, cacti and a fabulous Berber museum.
After ending the visit of this Garden, we will proceed to discover the new city with its palm-lined streets, red big buildings, cafes, boutiques and the luxurious modern life. We will then head for Menara Garden that contains olives groves, big pool and panoramic views of the Atlas Mountains. Afterwards, the tour goes back to the big square of Djemma lefna as a traditional meeting place for musicians, story tellers, snake charmers and entertainers etc. We will then make our way to Jardin Secret nestled inside the medina just few steps from the square.

Marrakech Museums

Itinerary –

Marrakech abounds in museums, and the diversity of museums is attributed to the content of each, ranging from Arabic and Berber heritage and cultural content such as Dar El Bacha, Dar Si Said Museum, Or African cultural content such as The Orientalist Museum, or artistic museums such as the Museum of the Photography which exhibits photos of Marrakech dating back to the 18th century.
Given the importance of gastronomy in Morocco and the richness of Moroccan cuisine, visitors of Marrakech can discover more about this art in the Museum of Culinary Art, they can even participate in the gastronomic workshops led by the museum staff.
The same thing for the Perfume Museum, which offers perfume-making workshops using ancient oriental methods and using artisanal equipment that the museum exhibits in its galleries.
There is also the Water Museum, which exhibits ancestral technology used in irrigation systems since the middle ages... Even a fashion and elegance museum is present in Marrakech thanks to the Yves St Laurent Museum!

Jewish Heritage Tour

Itinerary –

Visit the Jewish Mellah - Founded in 1558 by Moulay Abdallah, the Mellah district was designated as the Jewish quarter in Marrakech. At the time of the Spanish religious wars, Jewish refugees were escaping the country, and were offered this little piece of security by the Sultan
Visit the Lazama Synagogue in Marrakech, located in the old medina. The Lazama Synagogue dates back to the 16th century and is an important testament to Morocco’s deep Jewish history. This Quarter was created in the Kasbah area in 1558. there is a local legend that the synagogue was built during the Second Temple period by Jews who had never lived in Eretz-Israel and had not witnessed the destruction of the Temple
Tour the Rabbi Hanania Hacohen Cemetery, the place of burual for Rabbi Mordekhai Ben Attar and Rabbi Pinhas Hacohen Azough, where the “patron of Marrakech” resides
The Jewish community enjoyed autonomy even though Jews weren't allowed to own any property outside the Mellah and controlled the sugar trade. There are approximately 250 Jews still living in Marrakech, and most live outside the medina. The Mellah in the historic Marrakech medina is now almost completely Muslim. Visit The Beit El synagogue in the Villa Nouvelle of Gueliz

We leave Marrakech towards Ouarzazate cutting through the High Atlas Mountains via a well known and picturesque pass. The panoramic views are spectacular as we pass natural landscapes, traditional villages and the kasbahs typical of the Higher slopes of the mountains. We will stop to visit Kasbah Ait Ben Haddou and tour its archetecturally interesting kasbah set amongst the palms.
We continue towards Ouarzazate, where there are optional visits, such as Taourite Kasbah, or you may prefer to linger longer over lunch at your chosen restaurant. Later in the afternoon we will travel back to Marrakech reaching your hotel around 7pm.
